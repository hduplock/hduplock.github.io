About Malta

Malta is a civilization that dates back thousands of years. The country has some of the oldest megalithic sites in the world! Malta is situated in the center of the Mediterranean, with its islands having served as a strategic military asset.

Throughout its history, the Maltese islands have come under control of the:

  • Phoenicians
  • Carthaginians
  • Greeks
  • Romans
  • Byzantines
  • Moors
  • Normans
  • Sicilians
  • Spanish
  • Knights of St. John
  • French

After Malta was a British colony from 1814-1964, they declared their independence and declared itself a republic in 1974. And since the mid-1980’s the country has become a freight transshipment point, a financial center, and a tourist destination.

Finally, Malta became a member of the EU in May of 2004 and started using the euro as its currency four years later in 2008. The population as of July 2021 is 460,891.

Economy

Malta’s economy is the smallest economy of the countries that use the euro. It’s free market economy relies heavily on trade for both goods and services. The country only produces less than 25% of its food needs, also has limited fresh water supplies and has little domestic energy sources. The economy is reliant on foreign trade, manufacturing and tourism.

GDP

The countries Real GDP(purchasing power parity) ranks relatively low at 149 out of 195 countries due to its small size.

In 2020 the Real GDP was $20.6 billion.

However its Real GDP growth rate ranked high, coming in at 49 out of 195 countries, with a 4.94% growth rate.

Finally, the Real GDP per capita ranked even higher at 44, with a per capita rate of $39,200

Labor Force

Malta’s labor force ranks at 166 out of 195 countries. However despite the low ranking, over half the population is in the work force, with 233,000 out of the 460,891 inhabitants contributing to the labor force.

The countries industry is made up of agriculture (1.6%), industry (20.7%), and services (77.7%).

The unemployment rate ranks extremely high in the country comparison to the world coming in at number 5 of 195 with an unemployment rate of 0.78% in 2019.

Telecomunication

Malta has one of the most advanced telecom systems in Europe due to its favorable landscape, and tech savvy consumers.

The country has expansive mobile and broadband networks, has seen huge growth in e-commerce, and has allowed for extensive FttP network and investment in LTE and fiber until 2023.

Internet Users/Broadband

Internet

The country has 380,300 internet users, which is 164th in the world, out of 195 countries. Once again despite the small ranking, this is still 86.86% of the population connected to internet.

Broadband

There are 213,419 broadband users in the country with 48 subscriptions per 100 inhabitants. This ranks in at 115th in the world.

Phones/Landlines

Fixed Lines

Malta has 256,838 users of fixed telephone lines with 59 subscriptions per 100 inhabitants. This also ranks in at 115th in the world.

Mobile Ceullular

However there are 634,386 users of mobile cellular with 143 subscriptions per 100 inhabitants. This ranks in at 167th in the world.

Broadcast Media

Malta has two publicly owned TV stations, the Television Malta which broadcasts nationally plus an education channel. There are also several privately owned national tv stations, with two of them being owned by political parties.

Multi-channel cable and satellite TV are available as well as Italian and British tv programs.

The publicly owned radio operates three stations and there are roughly 20 commercial radio stations.

Military

The Maltese military is known as the AFM, Armed Forces of Malta. The AFM included land, maritime and air efforts as well as a Volunteer Reserve Force. The AFM currently has around 2,000 active duty personnel.

Malta also maintains a policy of neutrality but does contribute to the EU and UN military missions and also joined NATO’s Partnership for Peace program.

Expenditures

Malta doesn’t sound much of its GDP on the military, with only .5% going to military expenditures. Compared to the world, Malta ranks at 157 out of 195 countries.

Service/Obligation

There is no mandatory service in the country of Malta, with those who are 18-30 years old being able to volunteer. The country also does not have a mandatory draft or conscription.

Equipment

The inventory of the Armed Forces of Malta is relatively small. It consists of equipment from a bunch of European countries, mainly Italy, and also from the United States of America.
